# Alessandra Buonanno Open-Resource Map

Last checked: 2026-06-23

## Scope and method

This is a reproducible map of openly reachable material authored by, featuring, or officially describing the work of Alessandra Buonanno. It is comprehensive by **resource class**, not a claim to enumerate every collaboration paper, seminar appearance, mirror, or future publication.

The canonical way to capture the evolving publication record is to use the live indexes below. The curated papers emphasize the methodological arc relevant to this repository: analytic approximation, resummation, numerical-relativity calibration, detector connection, statistical inference, validation, and extension to new regimes.

Source priority:

1. official institutional pages and course materials;
2. arXiv, INSPIRE, LIGO DCC, and publisher/open-repository records;
3. first-party event and lecture pages;
4. interviews from established scientific institutions.

No inference about private supervisory practice should be made from this file. The prompt profile's pedagogical rules are explicitly a synthesis from public artifacts.

## What was distilled from the corpus

The repository's prompt profile draws only these defensible methodological inferences:

- combine complementary approximations and simulations rather than extrapolating one method without checks;
- state regimes of validity and connect theoretical error to observational performance;
- organize work from source dynamics through waveform, detector response, and inference;
- validate calibration against independent or held-out information;
- expose intermediate reasoning and use exercises that connect derivation, computation, and comparison;
- treat future sensitivity as a demand for tighter control of systematic error.

These are patterns in the public material, not claims about Buonanno's private supervisory manner.
